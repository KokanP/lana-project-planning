2. Wealth Concentration Analysis

This section analyzes the distribution of LANA among the Top 1000 richest addresses based on the `q=rich` API call.

  • Top 10 Holders (% of Circulating): 35.62%
  • Top 100 Holders (% of Circulating): 73.01%
  • Gini Coefficient (Top 1000): 0.813 (Calculated from Lorenz Curve based on `q=rich` data)

Interpretation

The concentration percentages are extremely high: the Top 10 addresses hold over 35% and the **Top 100 hold over 73%** of all circulating LANA. This indicates a significant concentration of wealth in a very small number of wallets. Such concentration can increase the risk of market manipulation and volatility, as decisions made by a few large holders ("whales") can disproportionately affect the price and liquidity available to smaller participants.

The Gini coefficient provides a single number to measure inequality (0 = perfect equality, 1 = perfect inequality). The calculated value of **0.813** is very high, confirming a severe imbalance in wealth distribution among the top addresses listed by the API. High economic inequality can be detrimental as it may discourage broader participation, reduce the velocity of money if large holders remain passive, and raise concerns about the decentralization and fairness of the ecosystem. For more context on whale influence, see the main analysis page.
